“Cookies” Information: We and our operational service providers and others who host the Delesin Site often use technology that can recognize, collect and/or transmit information that is associated with you, but which do not personally identify you. Although the term “cookie” specifically refers to a small text file that is stored on a User’s computer for record-keeping purposes, we use the term “cookie” to refer generically to any technology that can recognize, collect and/or transmit information about your visits to the Delesin Site. This type of information includes such data as the Internet or web domain that referred you, the type of operating system and browser you are using, the date and time of your visit, data relating to activities on the Delesin Site (e.g., so-called ‘clickstream’ data) and other technological attributes about your visit to the Delesin Site. This information is used to analyze trends, administer the Delesin Site, track user movements on the Delesin Site, and gather aggregate demographic information about visitors so we can continually improve the Delesin Site. Cookies also allow the Delesin Site to recognize your computer or access device each time you visit and cookies can keep count of how often you return. Cookies can also track how you use the Delesin Site and your preferences for features and functions, and cookies can store information such as your user name and password so that you don’t have to re-enter them each time you return to visit. Cookies don’t personally identify you or any particular user or individual.

 

When you visit the Delesin Site, we may send one or more cookies – a small text file containing a string of alphanumeric characters – to your computer. Delesin uses both session cookies and persistent cookies. A persistent cookie remains after you close your browser and may be used by your browser on subsequent visits to the Delesin Site. By using these cookies, we can “remember” what you have done on the Delesin Site before and personalize our site for you. These persistent types of cookies can be removed. You can disable or turn the cookie feature off by changing the settings on your Internet browser and you can also change the settings to stop your browser from automatically accepting cookies. Each web browser implements different functionality, so please refer to the manuals or technical support resources that are available in conjunction with your web browser to learn the correct way to modify your cookies set-up. If you are using the Delesin Site in a jurisdiction where your prior consent is required for us to deploy any particular cookies, you will be asked to accept such cookies before they are deployed. Please note that disabling cookies or declining to accept certain cookies will prevent you from accessing some of the functionality and site offerings available via the Delesin Site, and may also require you to re-enter certain information each time you visit or attempt to use the Delesin Site.
