Portfolio Analysis: An Abridged History

Selecting the members of a highly advantageous equity investment portfolio is a complex undertaking.  The portfolio designer must consider the benefits of risk diversification as well as expected returns.  In addition, determining the most powerful allocation requires using quantitative optimizations methods.  Finally, there are a variety of individual security, market, and economic considerations that must be addressed at each step.

 

Portfolio Diversification

Placing all of your resources in one investment invites otherwise avoidable risk.

Nowhere is the importance of economic diversification more eloquently expressed than in Shakespeare’s the Merchant of Venice (1599):

Antonio: Act 1, Scene 1 of The Merchant of Venice

Believe me, no. I thank my fortune for it —

My ventures are not in one bottom trusted,

Nor to one place, nor is my whole estate

Upon the fortune of this present year.

Therefore my merchandise makes me not sad

 

While diversification may reduce risk, it wasn’t until 1952 that a formal methodology was developed by H. Markowitz to minimize risk in a diversified portfolio of securities. Since then considerable improvements, modifications and adaptations have emerged under the general heading of portfolio optimization.
